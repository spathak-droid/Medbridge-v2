# MedBridge: Comprehensive Web Research - Reviews, Complaints & Pain Points

**Research Date:** March 2026
**Sources:** App Store reviews, Google Play reviews, JustUseApp, G2, Capterra, SelectHub, Software Advice, Trustpilot, PMC/NIH research papers, PT blogs, competitor comparisons

## 1. APP STORE REVIEWS (MedBridge GO - Patient App)

### Overall Ratings
- **iOS App Store:** 4.8/5 stars, 134,000+ ratings
- **Google Play:** 4.9/5 stars, 100K+ reviews

Despite high average ratings, detailed review analysis reveals significant, recurring pain points.

### Critical Patient Complaints (Extracted from actual reviews)

#### A. Timer & Exercise Pacing Problems (MOST COMMON)
- "Knee extensions supposed to be held for 3-5 minutes, but the program gives me 10 seconds"
- Timer calculations are mathematically wrong - exercises assigned incorrect durations
- Timer is keyed to video timings rather than actual exercise needs
- Program moves through exercises much quicker than it actually takes to do them
- Rest time between exercises too short, especially if users need to change equipment
- Hold times unsupported - the system doesn't account for prescribed hold durations (like "hold 5 seconds")
- Hands-free workouts impossible due to timer design

#### B. Data Loss & Logging Issues
- "Where's my data?" - Multiple reports of activity histories disappearing after updates (3.5 months of data lost in one case)
- Cannot delete or edit logged activities
- App logs save whether or not users hit the save button
- Deselected exercises still being logged as done
- Exercises marked complete without user logging them, creating false appearance of missed/completed sessions
- "If you try to log your activity and do it wrong you can't delete nor can you edit it"

#### C. Authentication & Connectivity
- "The biggest irritation is that the app keeps logging me out; re-authenticating on the old iPad I keep in our exercise rooms takes forever"
- Frequent unexpected logouts requiring re-authentication
- If users don't have internet, streaks are not counted even if they do the exercises
- App loses track of screen when switching apps or phone goes to sleep

#### D. Customization Gaps
- Therapists cannot specify exercise duration, only reps
- No way to input just an amount of time for exercises
- Cannot designate which arm/side for asymmetrical exercises
- Unable to set custom rep counts and rest periods
- No ability to focus on specific exercises during sessions
- Limited exercise selection flexibility

#### E. Accessibility & UX Issues
- Poor VoiceOver support
- Pause button unavailable in landscape mode
- Synthetic voice described as "horrible and makes me want to scream"
- No audio volume control
- Interface "blocking the screen" during reviews
- Bluetooth/AirPlay issues causing video pauses
- Exercise sequencing won't save

#### F. Representative Negative Reviews
- **"Sloppy, rushed app" (1 star):** Missing basic functions, no set/rep controls, timer crashes on sleep mode
- **"A chore to use" (2 stars):** Login failures, exercise switching bugs, errors accumulate after updates
- **"Some days it just doesn't work" (1 star):** Exercises marked complete without logging, sync issues, requires reinstallation
- **"Better than nothing but seriously flawed" (1 star):** Hold times unsupported, sequences don't save, app freezes after pausing
- **"I hate this app so much!" (1 star):** General frustration with accumulated issues

### Developer Response
Acknowledged plans to develop edit/delete for logged activities but noted "it's a complicated architecture due to how adherence reporting works."

## 2. CLINICIAN/PROVIDER REVIEWS (G2, Capterra, SelectHub, Software Advice)

### Ratings Summary
- **G2:** 4.3/5 stars (7 verified reviews)
- **Capterra:** 4.7/5 stars (16 reviews)
- **SelectHub:** 4.8/5 overall

### What Clinicians Love

1. **Continuing Education (Top Strength)**
   - 2,700+ courses, great quality, respected instructors
   - "Best video-based CEUs available in terms of both quality and selection"
   - Users remain subscribers for years

2. **HEP Builder**
   - 8,000+ exercise library with video demos
   - Easy to create and assign programs
   - Reduces paper waste
   - EMR integration syncs patient data

3. **Patient Compliance Tracking**
   - Can verify when patients actually do their exercises
   - Addresses misreporting of adherence

### What Clinicians Hate

1. **Constant Auto-Logouts (MOST CITED COMPLAINT)**
   - "Creating a home program and walking out to the gym results in being logged out and the program disappearing"
   - "I log into MedBridge more often than all other software I use combined"
   - Auto-logout every ~30 minutes for security
   - Causes loss of work in progress

2. **Exercise Database Limitations**
   - Doesn't always have the exact exercise clinicians want
   - "Somewhat challenging to modify exercise instructions on the site"
   - Relies heavily on pre-built library vs. custom content creation
   - Hard to add custom exercises with custom instructions

3. **Tedious Data Entry**
   - "Filling out details such as reps and sets can be tedious for each exercise"
   - Copying text into patient charts copies all details rather than just exercise titles
   - Makes chart review "messier" than necessary

4. **Reporting Limitations**
   - Difficult to track progress and measure training effectiveness
   - Cannot generate detailed reports on individual learner performance
   - Limited analytics compared to competitors

5. **Multiple Care Plan Navigation**
   - "A bit tricky when you have multiple care plans from different providers"
   - Hard to switch between care plans or look at past care plans

6. **Pricing Concerns**
   - $275-455/year per individual clinician
   - Enterprise: $2,400-$9,600/year for 10-user practice
   - Higher cost barrier for solo practitioners and small practices
   - Competitor AC Health starts at $14-60/month

## 3. REDDIT & FORUM DISCUSSIONS

Direct Reddit results were limited in search, but PT community feedback gathered from forums, blogs, and discussion platforms reveals:

### Physical Therapist Sentiment
- **Overall positive** about CEU quality and content
- **Frustrated** with workflow interruptions (logouts)
- **Concerned** that patients often don't actually use the app
- The "I forgot" excuse remains common even with app reminders
- Some older/less tech-savvy patients simply won't use it
- **Value proposition questioned** for small practices due to cost

### Student Doctor Network Forums
- Valued for continuing education
- Quality comparable to or better than alternatives
- Pricing is a consideration but generally considered worth it

### Blog Reviews from PTs
- **John Snyder, DPT** (detailed review): Timer-based progression is biggest weakness - patients perform movements at varying speeds causing them to feel behind schedule. Isometric holds are poorly supported. Still endorsed overall.
- **OT Potential, OT Miri:** Positive on CEU content, less focus on patient app issues
- **Skip & Jazz Johnson (travel PT):** Recommended as "best PT online CEUs and HEP builder"

## 6. THE HEP ADHERENCE CRISIS - Research Literature

### Adherence Rates (The Core Problem)

| Metric | Rate | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Patients fully adhering to plans of care | **35%** | Multiple studies |
| Non-adherence for MSK conditions | **50-65%** | Systematic reviews |
| Non-adherence for low back pain | **50-70%** | Condition-specific studies |
| Patients abandoning programs within first month | **65%** | Sprypt/research compilation |
| Patients failing to complete full course of care | **70%** | WebPT/Marquette University |
| HEP adherence at lowest point | **As low as 30%** | PMC systematic review |
| Self-discharge before completing care plan | **55%** | Chronic knee/back pain study |

### Why Patients Don't Do Their Exercises

#### Strong Evidence (Multiple High-Quality Studies, 6+ trials)
1. **Low self-efficacy** (6 trials, 1,296 participants) - "I don't believe I can do this"
2. **Poor social support** (6 trials, 2,286 participants) - No encouragement from family/friends
3. **Depression** (4 trials, 1,367 participants) - Mental health barrier
4. **Low baseline physical activity** (4 trials, 728 participants) - Not active before therapy
5. **Greater perceived barriers** (3 trials, 857 participants) - "Too many obstacles"
6. **Pain during exercise** (2 trials, 159 participants) - Fear of worsening condition

#### Patient-Reported Barriers
- **50% cite "forgetting"** as primary barrier
- **37% identify "lack of time"** as main obstacle
- Inability to fit exercises into daily routine
- Work schedules and transportation issues
- Feeling discouraged when progress isn't visible

#### Clinical/System Barriers
- Breakdown between supervised sessions and unsupervised home exercise
- Lack of follow-up and difficulty contacting providers
- Number/complexity of prescribed exercises (more exercises = lower adherence)
- Paper handouts as sole reference material

#### Psychological Barriers
- Anxiety/stress (2 trials, 159 participants)
- Helplessness (2 trials, 792 participants)
- External locus of control
- Pessimism during pain episodes
- Fear of re-injury (can extend recovery by 50-75%)

### Financial Impact of Non-Adherence
- **70% of patients** don't complete full course of care
- **20% drop out** within first three visits
- **35% cite financial barriers** as factor in discontinuation
- Acquiring new patients costs **5-25x more** than retaining existing ones
- Patients with poor HEP adherence are **60% more likely to discontinue therapy**
- Each lost patient = lost revenue from incomplete treatment plan

### What Improves Adherence (Research-Backed)

| Intervention | Impact |
|---|---|
| Written + illustrated instructions vs. verbal only | 77.4% vs 38.1% adherence |
| Solution-focused brief therapy techniques | 40% improvement in completion rates |
| App-based reminders | Nearly all study participants exercised more regularly |
| Therapist monitoring of performance | Higher adherence when patients know they're tracked |
| Fewer exercises prescribed | Inverse relationship: fewer exercises = higher adherence |
| Gamification elements | Measurable improvement in engagement |
| Reward-incentive gamification app | 7.79 vs 4.58 kept appointments |
| Pop-up reminders + completion tracking | Redirected patient focus to exercises |

### What Patients Want in an Exercise App (Research)
1. **Video and text instructions** (ranked highest in studies)
2. **Reminder functionality** (push notifications at right times)
3. **Self-monitoring/progress tracking**
4. **Communication with therapist** between visits
5. **Pain and difficulty reporting**
6. **Personalized adjustments** based on feedback
7. **Exercise streaks and gamification**
8. **Accessibility across devices**

### Key Research Insight
> "There is no gold standard for the measurement of adherence to unsupervised home-based exercise." Most studies rely on self-report measures prone to recall and bias. - PMC Systematic Review

> All 17 patients in a qualitative study preferred mobile apps over paper handouts, but 50% of therapists preferred continuing paper handouts, citing workflow disruption concerns.

- **Behavioral nudging** based on evidence-based adherence research

## 8. KEY TAKEAWAYS FOR PRODUCT DEVELOPMENT

### The Opportunity
1. MedBridge has massive market penetration (360K+ providers, 90M+ programs) but **the patient engagement layer is fundamentally passive**
2. The app records and tracks but does not actively coach, motivate, or intervene
3. 65% of patients abandon their programs within the first month - this is the #1 problem to solve
4. 50% of patients cite "forgetting" as their barrier - solvable with smart reminders
5. Low self-efficacy is the strongest predictor of non-adherence - an AI coach can address this directly
6. The technical debt in MedBridge GO (timer bugs, data loss, logging issues) creates user frustration that compounds the adherence problem
7. Clinicians love the content but hate the workflow interruptions - a better provider experience would drive adoption

### What an AI Coach Should Address
Based on this research, the ideal intervention would:
- **Proactively reach out** when patients miss exercises (not wait for them to open the app)
- **Address forgetting** with smart, contextually-timed reminders
- **Build self-efficacy** through encouragement, milestone celebration, and progress visualization
- **Reduce perceived barriers** by simplifying programs and offering modifications
- **Screen for depression/anxiety** and escalate to clinician when needed
- **Adapt exercise pacing** to actual patient capability (not fixed timers)
- **Provide offline support** so progress isn't lost without connectivity
- **Enable easy communication** with the care team
- **Use gamification** thoughtfully (streaks, rewards, social support)
- **Predict dropout risk** and trigger early intervention
